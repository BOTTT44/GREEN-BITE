BMR Definition: Your Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR) is the number of calories you burn as your body performs basic (basal) life-sustaining function. Commonly also termed as Resting Metabolic Rate (RMR), which is the calories burned if you stayed in bed all day. In either case, many utilize the basal metabolic rate formula to calculate their body’s metabolism rate.

  • The beating of our heart
  • Cell production
  • Respiration
  • The maintenance of body temperature
  • Circulation
  • Nutrient processing

BMI Definition: Body Mass Index (BMI) is a measure of body fat based on your weight relative to your height. It helps determine if you are underweight, normal weight, overweight, or obese, giving an idea of your overall health risk related to body weight.

BMI Definition: Body Mass Index (BMI) is a simple calculation using your height and weight to estimate body fat and categorize your health risk. While BMI does not directly measure body fat, it helps identify whether you are underweight, normal, overweight, or obese. Unlike BMR, which measures how many calories your body burns at rest, BMI reflects your body composition. Together, BMR and BMI provide a clearer picture of your overall health and energy needs.
